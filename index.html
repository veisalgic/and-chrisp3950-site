Our Story

A Family Recipe,
Fifteen Years in the Making

Miller's Bakery was founded in 2009 by James and Clara Miller, a husband-and-wife team who left careers in finance and graphic design to pursue the one thing they loved more than anything else: the smell of fresh bread in the morning. James had spent years refining his sourdough technique in their small Maplewood apartment, gifting loaves to neighbors until the demand grew too big for a home oven. Clara designed the original logo on a napkin the night they signed the lease on their first 400-square-foot shop on Elm Street.

What started as a two-person operation with one commercial oven and a secondhand display case has grown into a beloved community institution with a team of eight bakers, a pastry chef, and a front-of-house crew that knows most regulars by name. Head pastry chef Dominique Reyes joined in 2014, bringing French patisserie training from the Ferrandi School in Paris and elevating Miller's cake and croissant program to something truly extraordinary.

Our mission has never changed: bake honest food from real ingredients, charge fair prices, and make our neighborhood a little sweeter every day. We source our dairy from Sunrise Valley Farm in Morris County, our eggs from Blackwood Hollow Chickens just 12 miles away, and our flour from a heritage grain mill in upstate New York. Nothing artificial, no shortcuts, no apologies.

We believe a great bakery is more than a place to buy bread — it's a gathering place, a slow-down-and-breathe place, a place where you run into your neighbor and share a table. Miller's is open seven days a week because good baking deserves no days off. James still shapes every sourdough boule himself each morning, arriving at 4am without complaint. That dedication is baked into every loaf we sell.
