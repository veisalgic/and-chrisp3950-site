Our Story

Apple Creek Creamery was born in 2011 when founders Tom and Linda Miller decided to turn their third-generation dairy farm into something the whole community could enjoy. After years of selling raw milk and cream to local cheesemakers, Tom suggested they try their hand at small-batch ice cream — and the first weekend they opened, they sold out in two hours.

What started as a weekend project at the end of the farm lane quickly grew into a beloved seasonal destination. Word spread fast. Families started driving from neighboring towns. School field trips were booked out weeks in advance. By 2014, Tom and Linda had built the dedicated creamery stand that still stands today — same location, same recipes, same smiling faces.

Our mission has always been simple: use the freshest ingredients we can get our hands on, treat every scoop like it matters, and make Apple Creek a place where families want to return year after year. We source fruit from nearby farms, honey from Sunnyside Apiary just three miles away, and of course, all our cream comes straight from our own herd of Holstein cows right here on the property.

We believe in transparency, quality, and community. Every flavor is made in small batches — never mass-produced, never frozen in advance. When you taste Apple Creek ice cream, you taste the season. That's a promise we've kept since day one, and one we intend to keep for generations to come.
