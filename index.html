Our Story

Three Generations of Flour, Butter & Heart

Miller's Bakery was founded in 1987 by Harold and Dorothy Miller, who had spent years dreaming of opening a neighborhood bakery that felt like an extension of their own kitchen. Harold, a trained pastry chef who had apprenticed in Lyon, France, combined classical technique with Dorothy's instinct for warmth and community. They opened their first location on 412 Elmwood Avenue with $6,000 in savings, a secondhand deck oven, and an unwavering belief that great bread could change a morning.

In 2003, their daughter Claire Miller-Voss took over day-to-day operations and expanded the menu to include celebration cakes, seasonal pastries, and a full café line. Claire brought a modern sensibility to the recipes while fiercely protecting the traditions her parents had built. Under her leadership, the bakery won the Maplewood Small Business Award four years running and grew from a staff of three to a team of nineteen passionate bakers and baristas.

Our mission is simple: use the best ingredients we can source, waste as little as possible, and make every customer feel genuinely cared for. We partner with Sunnyside Farm in Westbrook for eggs and dairy, Ridgeline Mills for our heritage-grain flours, and a rotating selection of local fruit growers to keep our seasonal offerings honest and fresh.

We believe a great bakery is a kind of anchor for a neighborhood — a place where regulars are known by name, where the smell of fresh bread signals safety and comfort, and where celebration and ordinary Tuesday mornings deserve equal joy. We're so grateful you've found us, and we can't wait to bake something special for you.

👩‍🍳

Claire Miller-Voss

Head Baker & Owner
